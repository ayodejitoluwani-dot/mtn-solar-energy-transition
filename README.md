# MTN Nigeria — Solar Energy Transition Analysis

A data-driven financial case for transitioning MTN Nigeria's 
cell tower and office infrastructure from diesel to solar energy.

## The Business Problem
MTN Nigeria spends an estimated ₦8.49 billion annually on diesel 
across 500 cell towers and 80 offices. This project quantifies 
the financial and environmental case for solar conversion.

## Key Findings
- ₦6.80B saved annually by switching to solar (80.1% of diesel cost)
- Full solar investment recovered in under 1 year
- ₦63.8B net saving over 10 years
- 15,200 tonnes of CO₂ eliminated annually

## Phased Rollout Recommended
| Phase | Timeline | Assets | Annual Saving |
|-------|----------|--------|---------------|
| Phase 1 — Quick Wins | Year 1 | 170 assets | ₦2.1B/yr |
| Phase 2 — Northern Scale-Up | Years 2–3 | 220 assets | ₦2.8B/yr |
| Phase 3 — Full Transition | Years 4–5 | 190 assets | ₦1.9B/yr |
